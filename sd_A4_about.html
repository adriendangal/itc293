About Me

Personal background, interests, goals, and reflection on my portfolio development.

Who Am I?

My name is Sachin Dangal. I am studying a Bachelor of Information Technology at Charles Sturt University in Australia. I am interested in technology because it can be used to build practical solutions for real people and businesses.

My current focus is improving my web design, programming, and problem-solving skills. I am also interested in AI tools, automation, and building digital services for small businesses.

Interests and Hobbies

  • Web design — learning how to create clean, professional websites.
  • AI tools — exploring how automation can improve business workflows.
  • Content creation — learning how digital content can build a personal brand.
  • Fashion and design — interested in minimal branding and visual identity.
  • Customer service — improving communication through hospitality work.
  • Investing — building early financial literacy through ETF investing.

Goals

My short-term goal is to improve my technical foundation in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, programming, and networking. I want to become more confident in building websites that are professional, accessible, and easy to maintain.

My long-term goal is to use technology and AI to create useful digital products and services. I am especially interested in practical systems that help small businesses save time and respond to customers more effectively.

Reflection: What Changed from A3 to A4

A3 established the visual design, structure, and CSS system. For A4 I extended that foundation significantly without breaking the consistent look and feel. The main changes are:

  • Added three new skills pages: Communication, AI & Automation, and Problem Solving.
  • Upgraded the Web Skills page to include a JavaScript page (sd_A4_javascriptpage.html) with a button that opens the newsletter form in a new tab.
  • Created the newsletter signup form (sd_A4_newsletter.html) with all required fields, JS validation, onload alert, and pure CSS styling.
  • Moved all JavaScript to an external scripts.js file for reusability and cleaner HTML.
  • Extended style.css with form-specific styles and a responsive breakpoint.
  • Updated all page headers, footers, and nav bars to ITC293 Assessment 4.
  • Renamed all files to use the sd_A4_ prefix for clear assessment versioning.
