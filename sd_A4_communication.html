Communication Skills

How clear communication creates value for businesses and clients.

Why Communication Matters at NexaCore

Technology companies like NexaCore rely on clear communication to convert prospects into clients, retain existing customers, and keep internal teams aligned. An employee who can explain technical concepts in plain language — verbally and in writing — is a direct asset to the business.

I have developed communication skills across two environments: hospitality (high-volume, real-time customer interaction) and outbound sales (cold calling and email outreach for my AI receptionist project).

Examples of My Communication Experience

Customer-Facing Hospitality

Working front-of-house in a busy hospitality environment required me to communicate clearly and calmly under pressure. I regularly handled customer complaints, explained menu options, and worked with a team to deliver consistent service. This taught me how to read a room, adjust my tone, and resolve issues quickly.

Outbound Sales — Cold Calling

For my AI receptionist business, I conducted cold calls to dental clinics and real estate agents across Melbourne. I developed and refined a short pitch that communicated the value of AI phone answering to non-technical business owners. This required me to listen actively, handle objections calmly, and follow up professionally by email.

Written Communication

I write concise outreach emails, assessment reports, and documentation. I focus on clarity — short sentences, plain language, and a logical structure. These habits carry directly into writing professional emails, client proposals, and technical documentation in a workplace setting.
